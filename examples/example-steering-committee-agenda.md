# Example Steering Committee Agenda

This example illustrates how a steering committee meeting can be structured to support program governance.

Steering committees provide leadership oversight for complex programs. They review program health, address escalated risks, and approve major decisions that affect scope, timeline, or resources.

Steering committee meetings should focus on decisions and escalations rather than operational status updates.

---

## Steering Committee Purpose

The steering committee provides governance oversight for the program by:

- reviewing overall program health
- addressing escalated risks or blockers
- approving major decisions or trade-offs
- ensuring alignment with organizational priorities

Operational coordination and detailed status reviews should occur within the program leadership structure before issues are escalated to the steering committee.

---

## Typical Participants

Steering committee membership varies by organization, but typically includes:

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Executive Sponsor | Provides strategic direction and final decision authority |
| Steering Committee Members | Represent key business or technology stakeholders |
| Program Lead | Presents program status, escalations, and decision requests |
| Workstream Leads (as needed) | Provide context for escalated issues or decisions |

---

## Meeting Preparation

To keep steering committee meetings focused and efficient, the following preparation should occur before the meeting:

- program status reports reviewed by program leadership
- risks evaluated and mitigation plans proposed
- decisions clearly framed with recommended options
- supporting documentation prepared for leadership review

Steering committee meetings should focus on **decisions and escalations**, not routine operational updates.

---

## Example Steering Committee Agenda

| Agenda Item | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Program Health Overview | Provide a brief summary of overall program status |
| Major Milestone Updates | Highlight progress on key delivery milestones |
| Escalated Risks or Issues | Review risks that require leadership attention |
| Decision Requests | Present decisions requiring steering committee approval |
| Strategic Alignment | Confirm program alignment with business priorities |
| Next Steps | Confirm actions and follow-up items |

---

## Typical Steering Committee Decisions

Steering committees commonly approve or guide decisions related to:

| Decision Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Scope adjustments | Expanding or reducing program scope |
| Timeline changes | Approving milestone or phase schedule adjustments |
| Resource allocation | Assigning additional staffing or funding |
| Risk mitigation | Approving major mitigation strategies |
| Strategic alignment | Confirming continued alignment with business priorities |

---

## Escalation Handling

When risks or issues exceed the authority of program leadership to resolve, they should be escalated through the governance structure described in:

`docs/02-governance-model.md`

Steering committees may:

- approve mitigation strategies
- adjust program scope or timelines
- allocate additional resources
- escalate major issues to the executive sponsor
