
# Executive Reporting

This section describes how to communicate program health, progress, and major decisions to leadership.

Executive reporting should provide clarity, not noise. Strong updates focus on what leaders need to know to support the program and make informed decisions.

## Effective Executive Updates

Executive updates often include:

- overall status
- key achievements
- major risks or blockers
- decisions needed
- upcoming milestones
- impact on business outcomes

## Purpose of Reporting

Effective reporting helps leadership:

- understand program health
- remove blockers
- make timely decisions
- maintain confidence in execution

Good reporting is concise, consistent, and decision-oriented.
